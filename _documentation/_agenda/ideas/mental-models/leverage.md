# Leverage

A small input that produces a disproportionately large output. Archimedes: "Give me a lever long enough and I shall move the world."

In practice: find where effort multiplies, not just adds.

## Types of leverage (Naval Razikant's framing)
- **Labor**: other people's time (low leverage, high cost)
- **Capital**: other people's money (medium leverage)
- **Code**: software that runs while you sleep (high leverage)
- **Media**: content that distributes infinitely (high leverage)

## When to use
- Prioritization: of 10 things I could do, which one makes the others easier or irrelevant?
- Hiring: what skill, if I had it, would compound everything else?
- Architecture: what abstraction eliminates an entire class of future problems?

## Example
Writing a shared design system for your apps = high leverage. Now every future UI change is faster, consistent, and debuggable.

Fixing a root cause in the data pipeline = high leverage. Now all downstream reports are correct without touching them.

Automating a recurring manual task = leverage that compounds with each repetition.

## Pitfall
Leverage amplifies both good and bad. A high-leverage architectural decision that's wrong creates high-leverage problems.
