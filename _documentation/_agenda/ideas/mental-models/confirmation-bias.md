# Confirmation Bias

The tendency to search for, interpret, and remember information in a way that confirms what you already believe.

One of the most pervasive and costly cognitive biases. Affects everyone, including experts.

## When to use (watch for it)
- When validating a hypothesis — are you looking for disconfirmation?
- When reading user feedback — are you noticing only the positive reviews?
- When hiring — are you finding reasons to like the candidate you liked in the first 5 minutes?

## How to counter it
- **Steel-manning**: articulate the strongest version of the opposing view
- **Pre-mortem**: assume your idea fails — why?
- **Explicit disconfirmation**: before concluding, ask "what would have to be true for me to be wrong?"
- **Diverse sources**: seek out people who disagree with you and have good reasons

## Example
You build a feature and ship it. Users send feedback. You remember the 3 positive comments and forget the 12 neutral/negative ones. The feature "validated."
