# Availability Heuristic

We judge the probability of something by how easily an example comes to mind — not by actual frequency or data.

Vivid, recent, and emotionally charged events are overweighted. Quiet, common, statistical realities are underweighted.

## When to use (watch for it)
- Risk assessment: "I've never seen X fail" ≠ "X rarely fails"
- Feature prioritization: the loudest user complaint ≠ the most common problem
- Hiring/evaluation: recent performance ≠ overall track record

## Example
You hear about a startup raising a big round and feel like "everyone is raising money." In reality, the vast majority of startups don't raise. The few that do make the news.

A support ticket about a bug comes in twice in one week. It feels like a critical problem. Your data shows it affects 0.3% of users.

## How to counter it
- Look at base rates and data, not just memorable cases
- Ask: "is this vivid, or is it actually frequent?"
- Maintain logs and metrics rather than relying on memory
