# Supply and Demand

Price and value emerge from the intersection of scarcity (supply) and desire (demand). When supply exceeds demand, price falls. When demand exceeds supply, price rises.

One of the most universally applicable models — extends far beyond money.

## When to use
- Pricing: am I pricing based on my cost or based on demand/scarcity?
- Career: what skills are scarce and in demand? Where is the supply/demand curve favorable?
- Attention: what is the supply/demand curve for attention in this space?
- Time: your attention is scarce — what is demanding it? At what price?

## Example
Your skill is common (high supply of React devs) and in demand — decent market. Become rare (high supply of people who can do both React and ML integration) and the curve shifts dramatically in your favor.

Everyone is building mobile-first tools. Desktop power users are underserved. Demand exists; supply of quality tools is low. That's a market signal.

## Key insight
You can influence both sides. Differentiation reduces supply competition. Marketing increases demand. Both shift the curve.
