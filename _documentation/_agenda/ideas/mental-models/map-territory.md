# The Map Is Not the Territory

Every model, plan, or mental image is a simplified representation of reality — not reality itself. Confusing the two leads to bad decisions.

From Alfred Korzybski. Formalized in general semantics.

## When to use
- When a plan breaks down on contact with reality ("it worked in theory")
- When data disagrees with your mental model — update the model, not the data
- When you realize you're reasoning from assumptions, not from observation

## Example
A product roadmap is a map. The actual user behavior is the territory. When users don't follow the roadmap's implicit assumptions, the roadmap is wrong — not the users.

A financial model is a map. The real market is the territory. Models that "prove" a strategy works are only as good as their assumptions.

## Pitfall
You need maps — you can't operate without models. The point isn't to distrust models, but to remember they have edges and blind spots.
