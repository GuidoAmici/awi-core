# First Principles Thinking

Break a problem down to its most fundamental truths, then reason up from there — instead of reasoning by analogy from what others have done.

## When to use
- When you're stuck in conventional solutions and nothing is working
- When you want to build something genuinely new, not just an iteration
- When someone says "that's how it's always been done"

## Example
Elon Musk on rocket costs: instead of asking "how do we build cheaper rockets," he asked "what are rockets made of, and what do those materials actually cost?" — found the materials were ~2% of the market price, the rest was assumptions.

Applied to your work: instead of "how should a learning platform look," ask "what does a person need to actually retain a skill?" Everything else follows.

## Pitfall
Time-intensive. Don't apply to every decision — use for high-stakes, high-leverage problems only.
