# Opportunity Cost

The true cost of any choice is the value of the best alternative you gave up.

Every yes is implicitly a no to everything else. Opportunity cost makes the "no" explicit.

## When to use
- Prioritization: the question isn't "is this worth doing?" but "is this worth doing *instead of X*?"
- Time allocation: what is the best use of the next hour — what are you giving up?
- Hiring and delegation: what does doing this task myself cost in terms of what else I could do?

## Example
Building a feature in-house: cost is not just dev time, but the features you didn't build, the users you didn't talk to, the architecture you didn't improve.

Taking a meeting: cost is not just 1 hour, but the state of deep work you sacrificed.

Free tools: cost is not money, but data, attention, or lock-in.

## Pitfall
Opportunity cost paralysis. You can't optimize every decision this deeply. Reserve it for high-stakes choices: what to build, where to invest your best hours, strategic direction.
