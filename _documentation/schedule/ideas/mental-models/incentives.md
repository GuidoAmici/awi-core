# Incentives

Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome. Systems produce the behavior they reward, regardless of stated intent.

Charlie Munger: "Never, ever, think about something else when you should be thinking about the power of incentives."

## When to use
- Diagnosing broken team behavior: what does the current system actually reward?
- Designing products: what behavior does this feature incentivize at scale?
- Understanding institutions: why does this company do something that seems irrational?

## Example
Sales team is rewarded on closed deals, not retention. Result: salespeople oversell, customer success is overwhelmed, churn is high. The incentive structure produced this outcome — no one is "bad."

Product has a streak mechanic. Users open the app to protect the streak, not because they want to learn. Engagement metrics up; learning outcomes flat.

## Design principle
Before blaming people for bad behavior in a system, audit the incentives. Change the incentive, change the behavior.
