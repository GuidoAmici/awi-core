# Loss Aversion

Losses feel approximately twice as painful as equivalent gains feel good. People are more motivated to avoid losing $100 than to gain $100.

From Kahneman & Tversky's Prospect Theory (1979).

## When to use (watch for it)
- When you're holding onto a bad project/feature because dropping it "feels like giving up"
- When pricing or offering trials — framing as "don't lose this" outperforms "gain this"
- When you're avoiding a risk that has positive expected value

## Example
You've been working on a feature for 3 months and it's not working. Loss aversion says: keep going (we've invested so much). Rational analysis says: the past 3 months are sunk — evaluate future value only.

In UX: "You have 3 days left of your free trial" converts better than "Upgrade to get premium features" — it frames loss, not gain.

## Pitfall
Combining with sunk cost fallacy makes this especially dangerous. The combination = "I've already lost so much, I can't stop now." Recognize both together.
