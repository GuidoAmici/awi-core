# Emergence

Complex behavior arising from simple rules interacting — properties that no individual component possesses but the system as a whole does.

You cannot predict or understand emergent behavior by studying the parts in isolation.

## When to use
- When a system behaves in ways none of its components "explain"
- When you're building platforms or communities — what will users do with this?
- When debugging unexpected system behavior at scale

## Example
Each ant follows simple rules (follow pheromone trails, deposit when carrying food). The colony solves optimization problems no single ant understands.

Twitter's reply culture, meme propagation, pile-ons — none of these were designed. They emerged from the interaction of simple features (like, reply, retweet).

In your product: gamification elements interact with user psychology to produce behaviors (obsessive checking, streaks, social comparison) not present in any single feature.

## Design implication
You can't design emergent behavior directly — you can only design the conditions. Set the rules carefully; watch what emerges; adjust.
